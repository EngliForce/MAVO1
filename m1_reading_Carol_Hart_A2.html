📖 Story

Detective Carol Hart sat at her desk. She looked out the window. It was raining. Her hands were cold. Her coffee was cold too.

On her desk was a photo. In the photo, she was young and happy. She stood next to a red motorbike. Now, she didn’t ride anymore. Tomorrow would be her last day at work.

Carol wasn’t excited. Her pension was small, and she didn’t have much money. She wanted to travel to Italy or Greece, but she couldn’t afford it.

Someone knocked on the door. It was Officer Jake Bell. He was young and friendly.

“Detective Hart,” he said. “There was a break-in at the Goldman Gallery.”

“What’s missing?” Carol asked.

“A painting,” Jake said. “It’s called The Blue Storm. It’s very expensive.”

Carol knew the painting. It was worth more than three million pounds.

She stood up. Her knees hurt, but she was ready. “Let’s go,” she said. “I can solve one more case.”

At the gallery, the alarm had stopped for ten minutes. The back door was not broken.

“Someone knows the code,” Carol said. “It’s an inside job.”

They spoke to the security guard. He looked nervous. “I didn’t turn off the alarm,” he said.

Two days later, Carol and Jake sat in a café. “Do you remember Mrs. Baines, the cleaner?” Carol asked.

Jake nodded. “She said she was sick on Tuesday,” Carol said, “but the log says she was at the gallery.”

That night, Carol went to the gallery and talked to Mrs. Baines. The woman looked surprised, but she let her in.

In the interview room, Mrs. Baines told the truth. “My nephew needed money,” she said. “He told me the painting was a copy. But he took the real one.”

“Where is the painting now?” Carol asked.

“In a storage unit outside the city,” Mrs. Baines said.

One week later, the real Blue Storm was back in the gallery. Carol gave in her badge. Jake gave her a card. It said: You are the best. Enjoy Italy.

She smiled. She didn’t tell anyone, but the reward money went to her team. Now she could take a real holiday.

Three months later, Carol sat at a café in Rome. She drank a glass of cold white wine. The sun was warm on her face.

Jake sat across from her with a guidebook. “Ready to see the Colosseum?” he asked.

“In a minute,” she said. “This is the retirement I dreamed of.”
